= SPEC-001: Classical AI Reasoning Architecture: "Like One of Us"  
:sectnums:  
:toc:  

== Background  

The Classical AI Reasoning Architecture, "Like One of Us," aims to simulate human-like reasoning while adhering to ethical principles and human rights standards. It incorporates emergent cognition, accountability, and transparency to ensure fairness and alignment with societal values.  

By prioritizing compliance with the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) and addressing bias, explainability, and societal impact, the architecture is designed for equitable applications in domains such as healthcare, governance, and education.  

== Requirements  

### Must Have  
- **Core Reasoning Engine**:  
  - Data processing accuracy ≥99%.  
  - Pattern recognition precision and recall ≥95%.  
  - Predictions with RMSE ≤0.05.  
- **Emergent Cognition Modules**:  
  - Abstract reasoning for hypothesis testing and counterfactual analysis.  
  - Self-awareness with confidence scores ≥95%.  
  - Adaptive agency mechanisms.  
- **Ethical Framework**:  
  - Human Rights Impact Score (HRIS) aligned with UDHR principles.  
  - Explainability Score ≥85% using SHAP/LIME metrics and user feedback.  
- **Governance Mechanisms**:  
  - Real-time monitoring and a multidisciplinary ethics review board.  

### Should Have  
- **Bias Mitigation**:  
  - Reduction of gender, racial, and socioeconomic biases by ≥20%.  
  - Evaluation metrics such as disparate impact and equalized odds.  
- **Adaptive Ethical Frameworks**:  
  - Real-time updates based on societal feedback and system performance.  

### Could Have  
- Public-facing interfaces with ≥85% user satisfaction in transparency and usability evaluations.  
- Cross-domain adaptability for healthcare, education, and governance.  

### Won’t Have  
- Fully autonomous decision-making without human oversight.

  - Scoring Framework: Weighted rubric based on prioritized UDHR articles (e.g., non-discrimination, right to privacy).  
  - Evaluation by external human rights experts.  
  - Target: Score ≥90%.  

2. **Bias Mitigation**:  
  - Metrics: Disparate impact ratio ≥0.8, equalized odds improvement ≥20%.  
  - Evaluation Datasets: UCI Adult Income dataset, COMPAS recidivism scores.  

3. **Explainability Score**:  
  - SHAP/LIME Metric Target: ≥85%.  
  - User Study Outcomes: ≥85% comprehension and satisfaction from diverse user groups.  

4. **Environmental Impact**:  
  - Baseline energy consumption recorded during model training and evaluation.  
  - Tools: Energy-efficient frameworks (e.g., TensorFlow) and green cloud providers.  

5. **Societal Impact Assessment**:  
  - Stakeholder Feedback: Regular input from impacted communities.  
  - Long-Term Monitoring: Annual post-deployment impact assessments.

= SPEC-002: Quantum Plugin for AI Reasoning Architecture  
sectnums:  
:toc:  

== Background  

The Quantum Plugin extends the Classical AI Reasoning Architecture by leveraging quantum computing to solve high-dimensional problems, optimize decision-making, and enhance bias mitigation. The plugin focuses on demonstrating quantum advantage while addressing ethical, societal, and environmental considerations.  

This project integrates quantum and classical components into a hybrid architecture, with specific benchmarks for speed, accuracy, and fairness, ensuring ethical compliance and long-term sustainability.  

== Requirements  

### Must Have  
- **Quantum Reasoning Engine**:  
  - Optimization tasks with defined benchmarks (e.g., MaxCut and scheduling problems).  
  - Counterfactual analysis using quantum-enhanced techniques.  
- **Quantum Machine Learning (QML)**:  
  - Quantum-enhanced pattern recognition and feature selection.  
- **Ethical Framework Integration**:  
  - Quantum-assisted evaluation of human rights principles in decision-making.  

### Should Have  
- **Bias Mitigation**:  
  - Reduction of biases (e.g., gender, racial) using quantum techniques.  
  - Metrics: Equalized odds improvement and disparate impact ratio ≥0.8.  
- **Quantum State Monitoring**:  
  - Reliability metrics such as fidelity and coherence scores ≥95%.  

### Could Have  
- **Quantum Advantage Demonstration**:  
  - ≥10x speedup on specific tasks such as:  
    - MaxCut optimization for a graph with 50 nodes and defined constraints.  
    - Scheduling optimization for a 100-node network using QAOA.  
- **Energy Efficiency Enhancements**:  
  - ≥20% reduction in energy consumption compared to classical solutions.  

### Won’t Have  
- Full quantum-only decision-making without classical fallback mechanisms.
